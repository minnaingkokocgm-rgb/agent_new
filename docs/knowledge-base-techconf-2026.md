# TechConf 2026 — Knowledge Base

## Event Overview

TechConf 2026 is the premier technology conference of the year, taking place June 15-17, 2026 at the Moscone Center in San Francisco, California. The conference brings together over 5,000 developers, engineers, CTOs, and technology leaders from across the globe. This year's theme is "Building the Future: AI, Cloud, and Developer Tools."

The conference features three full days of keynotes, hands-on workshops, technical breakout sessions, and an expo hall with over 80 exhibitor booths. Attendees can explore the latest in artificial intelligence, cloud infrastructure, developer experience, open source, and cybersecurity.

## Keynote Speakers

Dr. Sarah Chen, VP of AI Research at Google DeepMind, will deliver the opening keynote titled "The Next Decade of AI: From Models to Agents." She will discuss how large language models are evolving into autonomous agent systems that can plan, reason, and execute complex workflows. Dr. Chen has published over 50 papers on reinforcement learning and was named one of TIME's 100 Most Influential People in AI.

Marcus Rivera, CTO of Vercel, presents "The Edge Revolution: Why Latency Matters More Than Ever." His talk covers edge computing trends, serverless architecture patterns, and how the next generation of web frameworks is pushing compute to the edge. He will demo new Vercel capabilities including edge-rendered AI inference.

Jennifer Okonkwo, Founder & CEO of DevTool AI, gives the closing keynote "Developer Experience is Product Experience." She argues that the tools developers use directly shape the products they build, and that investing in DX is the highest-leverage activity for engineering organizations. Her company DevTool AI raised $40M Series B in 2025.

## Session Tracks

### Track A: AI & Machine Learning
Covers LLM fine-tuning, RAG architectures, vector databases, AI agents, prompt engineering best practices, and responsible AI deployment. Sessions range from beginner-friendly introductions to advanced deep dives. Highlights include "Building Production RAG Systems with pgvector" by the Supabase team and "AI Agents in the Enterprise" by Anthropic.

### Track B: Cloud & Infrastructure
Covers Kubernetes at scale, multi-cloud strategies, infrastructure-as-code with Pulumi and Terraform, observability with OpenTelemetry, and cost optimization. The "Cloud Cost Horror Stories" panel features engineers from Netflix, Shopify, and Datadog sharing real-world lessons.

### Track C: Developer Experience & Tools
Covers IDE extensions, CI/CD pipeline design, code review automation, monorepo tooling, and API design. GitHub will unveil Copilot X integrations, and GitLab will demo their new AI-powered merge request reviewer.

### Track D: Open Source & Community
Covers sustaining open source projects, contributor workflows, license compliance, and community building. The Open Source Awards ceremony takes place on Day 2.

### Track E: Security & Compliance
Covers supply chain security, zero-trust architectures, SOC 2 automation, vulnerability scanning, and incident response. Snyk and Chainguard are leading workshops on software bill of materials (SBOM) management.

## Workshop Schedule

Monday June 15 (Day 1):
- 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM: "Building AI Agents with Laravel AI SDK" — Hands-on workshop where attendees build a complete AI agent that can search documentation, query databases, and generate reports. Prerequisites: PHP 8.4+, Laravel 13, basic AI concepts.
- 1:00 PM - 4:00 PM: "Vector Databases from Scratch" — Learn how vector similarity search works by implementing it yourself. Covers HNSW indexes, cosine similarity, and embedding models. Languages: Python and SQL.

Tuesday June 16 (Day 2):
- 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM: "Full-Stack TypeScript with Inertia.js v3" — Build a complete application using Laravel, Inertia, React 19, and TypeScript. Covers deferred props, prefetching, optimistic updates, and SSR.

## Exhibitor Booths

### Booth 101: DevTool AI
Product: AI-powered code review and testing platform. Features include automated PR summaries, test generation, and bug detection using fine-tuned code models. The team is offering live demos and a free 30-day trial for attendees. They are also hiring for senior full-stack and ML engineer positions. Notable customers include Stripe, Notion, and Linear.

### Booth 205: CloudPilot
Product: Multi-cloud deployment automation platform. CloudPilot uses AI to optimize infrastructure costs across AWS, GCP, and Azure. Their autopilot feature detects underutilized resources and suggests right-sizing. They claim average cost savings of 34% for enterprise customers. The booth features a real-time dashboard showing live cost savings across a simulated fleet of 10,000 VMs.

### Booth 312: OpenVector
Product: Open-source vector database built in Rust. OpenVector claims 10x faster queries than pgvector with full ACID compliance. They recently launched a managed cloud offering with a generous free tier (2GB storage, 100K queries/month). The booth has a live benchmark station where attendees can compare OpenVector against pgvector, Pinecone, and Weaviate.

### Booth 478: APIForge
Product: API design-first platform with automated SDK generation. APIForge generates client libraries in TypeScript, Python, Go, and Kotlin from OpenAPI specs. Their AI assistant can draft API specifications from natural language descriptions. They are launching a new feature called "API Observability" that tracks usage patterns and suggests optimizations.

### Booth 550: SecurityStack
Product: Dev-first security platform for startups. SecurityStack integrates with GitHub, Vercel, and AWS to provide continuous vulnerability scanning without slowing down deployments. Their "Security Score" metric gamifies security posture and is popular with engineering teams. They offer a startup plan at $99/month for teams under 10.

## Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Is there Wi-Fi at the venue?
A: Yes, the Moscone Center provides free high-speed Wi-Fi throughout the conference. Network name: TechConf2026, password: buildthefuture. The network supports up to 10,000 concurrent devices.

Q: Where can I find food?
A: The expo hall has a food court with 12 vendors offering diverse options including vegetarian, vegan, and gluten-free. Coffee stations are located throughout the venue. Lunch is included with all ticket types from 12:00 PM to 2:00 PM daily.

Q: How do I access session recordings?
A: All sessions are recorded and will be available on the TechConf portal within 48 hours. Gold and Platinum ticket holders get immediate access. General admission gets access 30 days after the event.

Q: Is there a mobile app?
A: Yes, download "TechConf 2026" from the App Store or Google Play. The app includes the full schedule, interactive venue map, networking features, and session bookmarking.

Q: What COVID-19 precautions are in place?
A: The venue follows current CDC guidelines. Hand sanitizer stations are placed throughout the venue. Masks are optional but welcome. If you feel unwell, please use the virtual attendance option included with all tickets.

Q: How do I contact the organizers?
A: Email info@techconf2026.com or visit the help desk in the main lobby. The help desk is staffed from 7:00 AM to 6:00 PM daily.

Q: Are there networking events?
A: Yes! The official after-party is Tuesday June 16 at 7:00 PM at the Exploratorium. There are also Birds of a Feather dinners organized by topic on Monday evening. Sign up via the mobile app.
